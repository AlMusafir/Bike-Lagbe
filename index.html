Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

1. Age: Helmets have a recommended lifespan, usually around 3 to 5 years from the date of purchase. Check the manufacturer's guidelines for the specific lifespan of your helmet.
2. Visible Damage: Inspect your helmet for any visible signs of damage, such as cracks, dents, or deformities. Even small cracks can compromise the helmet's ability to protect you.
3. Crash or Impact: If your helmet has been involved in any impact or crash, replace it immediately. Helmets are designed to protect you during one impact only and may not offer the same level of protection after a crash.

What are road bikes used for?

Road bikes are designed for riding on paved roads and are ideal for long-distance cycling, racing, and fitness riding. They have lightweight frames and thin tires for speed.

What are sports bikes?

Sports bikes, also known as sportbikes, are motorcycles designed for high-performance and agility. They have a more aggressive riding position with higher foot pegs and lower handlebars, allowing for better aerodynamics and cornering at high speeds. Sports bikes are often favored by enthusiasts and riders who enjoy spirited riding on paved roads or racetracks.

What sets naked bikes apart from other motorcycles?

Naked bikes, also referred to as streetfighters or standard motorcycles, are characterized by their minimalist design and lack of fairings (bodywork covering the engine). Unlike sports bikes, they offer a more upright and comfortable riding position, making them suitable for daily commuting and longer rides.
